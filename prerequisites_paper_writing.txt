1. Research area or Title
   ML-guided route selection vs cold-start in real-time carpooling: evidence from rider density experiments (NYC TLC data).

   Alternative short title: Warm-up vs cold-start route selection for carpooling profit: an empirical study.

2. Data and results which you have done
   - Data: NYC TLC taxi/rideshare-style data (drivers, riders, trips). Processed into ~1.8M driver trips, 1M riders; H3 spatial indexing; OSRM road-network routing (3 alternative routes per OD).
   - Training: 50K drivers → enhanced v2 dataset (108K rows, 38+ features) → LightGBM profit predictor (R² ~0.76, RMSE ~$8.44).
   - Experiments: 5,000 test drivers × 5 seeds, 5 strategies (cold-start, random, heuristic, ML warm-up, oracle). Run at 100%, 75%, 50%, 25%, and 10% rider density.
   - Results: (i) Warm-up beats cold-start at every density (paired t-test p ~ 3.5e-20 at 100%). (ii) Advantage increases at lower density: +$2.21 at 10% density (17.5% relative) vs +$1.10 at 100% (3.7%). (iii) Oracle gap $3.97; ML captures ~28%. (iv) Summaries, CSVs, and figures in results/ (extended_summary.txt, density_results.csv, density_advantage.png, baseline_comparison.png, etc.).

7. Methodology, objectives and research gap which you have done so far
   - Objective: Show that ML-guided route selection (“warm-up”) yields higher profit than default routing (“cold-start”) in carpooling, and that this advantage is larger at lower rider density.
   - Methodology: (i) Build enhanced training data (spatial/temporal/geometric features, no leaky match counts). (ii) Train LightGBM to predict profit per route. (iii) Simulation: for each test driver, generate 3 OSRM alternative routes; cold-start uses default route; warm-up uses ML to pick route with highest predicted profit; oracle picks route with highest actual profit (upper bound). (iv) Paired design (same drivers, riders, seeds); statistical tests (paired t-test, Wilcoxon, Cohen’s d, 95% CIs). (v) Rider density experiments (100%, 75%, 50%, 25%, 10%) to test when route choice matters most.
   - Research gap addressed: Prior work often focuses on matching or pricing; we focus on route selection given multiple alternatives and show that ML-based selection improves profit and that the gain is density-dependent (larger when rider supply is sparser).
